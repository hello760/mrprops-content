Last October, Airbnb bookings dried up overnight in Riga.

I had 6 units. Three of them sat empty most of November. Some weeks I'd get a 2-night booking at 50% of my summer rate. Most weeks, nothing.

Meanwhile the utility bills kept arriving. {{utility_burn_typical}} per month per unit. Latvia winters are heating-heavy. Empty apartments still need heat or pipes freeze.

By March I'd lost {{total_loss_last_winter}} across 3 units that should have been flipped to longer-term tenants by mid-September.

Three kilometers from one of those units, exchange students were paying {{exchange_student_rent_typical}}/month for a furnished 1BR — utilities included by the tenant.

I had the units. I had the location. I had the operator-side experience to make the lease work. I just didn't switch fast enough because the Airbnb dashboard made it look like I might still pull a booking week.

---

The lesson, in one line: **empty STR in winter isn't neutral — it's actively bleeding.**

If you're running a hybrid portfolio in any market with seasonal demand, October isn't when you decide whether to flip. It's when the flip needs to already be live.

Mid-September is the deadline. The next email walks through how I run that flip now.

— Helvis

---

P.S. The unit that lost the most? My Jūrmala studio. Right by the beach. Beautiful in summer. Negative-margin from October to March every single year I tried to keep it on Airbnb.
