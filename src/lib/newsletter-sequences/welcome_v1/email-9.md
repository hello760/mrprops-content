Airbnb has automatic verification, review history, identity confirmation, payment cards on file.

LTR and student rentals have none of that.

When I started flipping units to longer leases, I lost two months early on because I didn't have a screening framework. One tenant ghosted after first month. Another stayed but trashed the bathroom.

Here's the 4-question filter I use now. Goes through every applicant before I even show the place.

---

**Question 1: Proof of income or enrollment.**

For working-pro LTR: 3 months of payslips + employer letter. PDF. No exceptions.

For exchange students: official enrollment letter on university letterhead with start/end dates of their semester or program.

If they can't produce within 48 hours, they're not serious.

---

**Question 2: Reference, NOT a family member.**

A previous landlord. Or, for first-time renters, a workplace manager or university professor.

Family members are not references — they'll lie.

I always call. 30 seconds. Three questions: paid on time? respectful of the property? would you rent to them again?

You'd be surprised how often the reference hesitates on question 3. That's your signal.

---

**Question 3: Why this neighborhood specifically.**

This is the most important one and almost no operator asks it.

Good answer: "I work at [specific employer] 10 minutes away" / "the university campus is here" / "my partner lives in [adjacent district]" / "my kids go to school down the street."

Bad answer: "anywhere will do" / "I just need a place" / "no specific reason."

The bad answer means they're not committed. They'll move out at the first inconvenience. Pass.

---

**Question 4: What happens if you get a better job offer / move to another city.**

Most applicants pause on this one. That's fine.

What I'm filtering for: do they have a plan that doesn't involve breaking your lease?

Good answer: "I'd give 30 days' notice and pay through the notice period." Or "my contract is locked through summer."

Bad answer: "I'd just move" / "I haven't thought about that."

---

Plus the deposit pattern:

- Working-pro LTR: 1-month deposit + first month upfront
- Student rental: 2-month deposit + 2 months upfront, paid BEFORE key handover

Larger deposits on student leases because turnover damage risk is higher (younger demographic, often first time renting alone). Larger upfront because once they're in the country it's harder to chase missed payments.
