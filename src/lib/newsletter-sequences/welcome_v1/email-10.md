You've made it through the first arc.

Here's what I'd tell my year-one self if I could:

---

**Stop scaling. Start optimizing.**

I tried to grow to 12 units before I'd fixed per-unit profitability on the 6 I had. Every new unit just compounded the inefficiency. I ended up running back from 9 units to 6 — and the 6 net more now than the 9 did.

**Run the per-unit P&L every quarter, not every year.**

The Jūrmala unit was negative for 18 months before I noticed. Quarterly catches it in a quarter. Annual catches it after you've already lost the year.

**The seasonal flip is the unlock.**

In any heating-heavy market with student demand nearby, the {{exchange_student_rent_typical}}/mo + utility-pass-through play turns winter from cost center to clean income. Most operators don't do it because they don't know it works. Now you do.

**Don't fight the unit's natural fit.**

Some units want to be STR. Some want to be student rentals. Some want to be LTR. The 60% rule (Email 6) tells you which. Don't try to force a unit to be something it isn't because you read an article about scaling.

**Tenant screening matters more than tenant marketing.**

The 4-question filter from Email 9 has saved me two evictions and one trashed property in two years. Spend the time on the front end.
