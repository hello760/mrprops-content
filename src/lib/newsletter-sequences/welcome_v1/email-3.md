Yesterday I told you I lost {{total_loss_last_winter}} last winter.

Today: how I run the flip now so it doesn't happen again.

The mistake most operators make: they wait until bookings dry up to start switching. By then it's already costing them.

Here's the calendar I use:

**Mid-July:** Decide which units flip this year. Run the per-unit calc (next email). Lock the decision.

**Early August:** Stop accepting new Airbnb bookings past September 25 for those units. Block the calendar.

**Mid-August:** Post the listings on exchange-student housing portals + university Facebook groups + HousingAnywhere. Most international students arrange housing in August for September moves.

**Early September:** Sign 5-month leases with deposit + 2 months upfront. Why 5 months: matches one university semester. Either auto-renews for spring or you flip back to STR for spring/summer.

**Late September:** Last summer guests check out. Cleaner does turnover. Tenant moves in October 1.

---

A few things that make this work financially:

**Rent the unit furnished.** It's the leverage. {{exchange_student_rent_typical}}/mo for furnished is realistic — vs {{ltr_rent_typical}} for unfurnished open-market LTR.

**Pass utilities through to the tenant.** Lease specifies "tenant pays utilities directly to provider." This is what flips winter from "cost center" to "actual income."

**Deposit = 2 months for student leases, not 1.** Limits damage if they trash the place before the semester ends.

**Don't try to charge STR-grade nightly rate via the back door.** A 5-month lease at {{exchange_student_rent_typical}} clears more than 90 winter STR nights at {{str_winter_rate_typical}} ever will. Plus zero turnover, zero coordination tax.
