If you're tempted to "stick it out" with STR through winter — let me walk you through why the math defeats you.

**Summer:** {{str_summer_rate_typical}}/night, 70% occupancy, mostly clean turnovers, low utilities. A 1BR clears maybe {{str_summer_rate_typical}} × 21 nights ≈ €1,470/mo before fees. Net margin: healthy.

**Winter:** Daily rate drops to {{str_winter_rate_typical}}/night to even attract bookings. Occupancy drops to 30-40% if you're lucky. Same 1BR now grosses {{str_winter_rate_typical}} × 11 nights ≈ €385/mo.

Utilities: now {{utility_burn_typical}}/mo because heating.

So: monthly STR gross of €385 minus utilities of {{utility_burn_typical}}, before cleaning, listing fees, supplies, depreciation.

You're working full-time as a host, fielding 4-5 guest interactions per month, doing 3-4 cleanings, managing reviews — for almost zero margin. In some months, negative.

---

The tempting trap: "but at least I made €385 instead of zero."

That's wrong framing. The right comparison is:

- Stick with STR: ~€385 gross, minus ~€350 utilities, minus ~€80 cleaning, minus listing fees = roughly **negative €50/month** plus your time
- Flip to student-rental: {{exchange_student_rent_typical}}/month, tenant pays utilities, no cleaning, one-time setup = **{{exchange_student_rent_typical}} clean** with zero hours

You're choosing between net negative income (with stress) and clean positive income (passive). Stick-it-out STR isn't holding ground — it's losing on every dimension simultaneously.

---

The exception: flagship tourist locations. Old Town apartments in cities like Vienna, Prague, Riga. Ski-resort units near a major lift. Beach units in markets where "winter beach getaway" is actually a thing (Florida, Lisbon's December).

For those: winter STR demand stays high enough to clear utilities + margin. Keep them on STR year-round.

For everything else: flip or accept the loss.
