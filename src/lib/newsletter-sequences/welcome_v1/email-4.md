Three days ago: how I run the flip.

Today: where the tenants actually come from.

Most operators don't know exchange-student housing is its own market with its own channels. Once you know where they are, filling a unit takes a week, not a month.

Here are the four channels I use, in priority order:

---

**1. University international student housing offices.**

Every major university has one. They maintain off-campus housing lists for incoming exchange students. Some are full referral agencies, others just post a list.

Email the office in your city. Tell them you have a furnished unit, 5-month lease, walking distance to [campus / metro / their main building]. Ask to be added to their list.

This costs nothing. The university wants their incoming students housed safely. They'll add you.

**2. HousingAnywhere + Spotahome.**

Two paid platforms specifically for international students + relocators. Listings convert because the audience is pre-qualified — students with funded semesters, ready to lock a place from another country.

Listing fee is small. Worth it for September fills.

**3. City-specific Facebook groups.**

Search "[Your city] exchange students 2026/27" or "[Your city] Erasmus housing" (the EU program is called Erasmus, name doesn't matter — it's where students hang). Post the listing. Set notifications on for "ISO" / "looking for room" posts and DM directly.

**4. Existing student tenants' referrals.**

After the first year, this becomes your main channel. Your current tenant's friend wants to do a semester abroad next year. They DM you in February for the September lease. No marketing cost, pre-vetted by someone you already trust.

---

A few filtering rules I run on inbound applicants:

- Enrollment letter from the university (PDF, official letterhead). Not negotiable.
- One reference, not a family member.
- Move-in date matches my calendar block — no negotiation on that.
- 2-month deposit + first month upfront, paid before key handover.

That's it. Most students will pay this without negotiating because the alternative (trying to find housing in a foreign city) is harder.
